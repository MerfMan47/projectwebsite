Project Coaster

Like all great ideas this one started out for want of a gift to gift. I needed to make a Christmas gift for my grandparents and just so happened to be given some scraps of interesting wood by a friend who was into woodworking.

I decided on a simple square with rounded corners design. To determine the size I cut out several different sized prototypes out of plywood to physically assess the best ones. I believe the final product was 3.5 inches on each edge. As for the design I stuck with the idea that simplicity would be best. I found an elegant font and border to add to the svg files and put the wood in my universityâ€™s laser cutter. The one hiccup I encountered was that the wood was too dense and/or thick to be cut properly without burning the edges. An example of this failure can be seen in the second picture. I solved this issue by taking the pieces of wood to a chop saw then a belt sander to round off the edges manually.

The final step was to coat the coasters in three layers of water based polyurethane for durability and shine. Unfortunately I cannot find an image of the finished product.

The wood used for the K coaster was maple.

The wood used for the S & E coasters was walnut.

The wood used for the J coaster was ceder.

This project is in stage 6
