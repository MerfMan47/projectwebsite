Project Puget Sound Map

The project started out as a desire to get a Mother's Day gift for none other than my own mother. She enjoys adventuring around the Pacific Northwest and especially the Puget Sound area, taking photos, going on hikes, and visiting interesting places. One day she expressed interest in a topographical map of Puget Sound. Looking online I found they were quite small and expensive so I decided to make my own.

The plan was to contact the oceanography department at my school, the University of Washington, to acquire bathymetric data of Puget Sound, convert it into a Blender model, then slice it at specific intervals and expert each slice as an svg. However since I am not in the oceanography department I was having difficulty reaching the correct people. After a month of emailing and asking around I finally caved and bought a pattern off of Etsy. This turned out to be the correct choice as I was running out of time before the quarter ended. I acquired and cut quarter seven sheets of quarter-inch plywood to make the map's layers. Then I modified, sized, and prepared each layer file to work with my universityâ€™s laser cutter.

The first image is of one of the mid layers being cut.

After each layer was cut I took wood glue and vices to secure it to the main piece while the next layer was under the laser. Unfortunately I underestimated how long the final layer would take to cut and engrave. The laser cut the outline of each layer but the top layer needed to be engraved along with being cut. The engraving took approximately three hours alone and thus I was stuck waiting until 1:00am or so. This gave me a lot of time to think about planning projects and time management. Other than that everything went smoothly and I am now the maker of one of my mother's favorite pieces of art.

This project is in stage 6
