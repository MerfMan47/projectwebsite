Project Index Controller Joystick

I was playing VR one day only to find that Nintendo Switch controllers were not the only controllers that suffer from stick drift. I was hesitant to tear apart my only left hand Index controller so I waited until the drift became unbearable. This would also be my first high stakes soldering job. Fortunately new joystick assemblies were cheaply found on Amazon.

To get at the internals of the controller I had to pry of the top plastic covering of the controller then gently remove the ribbon cable for the thumb stick's touch sensor. Using additional solder and some wick I was able to remove the drifting joystick from the board. After which I cleaned up the remaining solder and carefully added more to secure the new joystick, slipped the thumb stick ribbon cable back in and snapped on the plastic covering.

Thankfully the controller was working perfectly after the repair retaining all touch sensitivity and with no more stick drift.

This project is in stage 6
