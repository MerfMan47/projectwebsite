Project The Great Divorce

Written by C.S. Lewis

The Great Divorce is an eye-opening short novel of less than 100 pages. The book follows a man who takes a bus up from Hell into Heaven with other sinners. Upon reaching Heaven the man chances listens to conversations between other sinners and the residents of Heaven. The residents of Heaven always try to convince the sinners to join them in Heaven but they seldom agree. Most choose to return to Hell citing reasons such as earthly desires, resentment, spitefulness, hatred of themselves and others, etc. Eventually the man learns that the wide expense of Hell is little more than a minuscule crack in the ground less an inch big showing that nothing in Hell can so much as challenged Heaven or the ones residing there. The book ends with the man being told that just like all the other sinners he has a choice to make to decide if, when his time comes, he will choose God and to stay in Heaven or remain trapped forever in Hell. Finally, the man wakes up in his own bed in his own home after being told to tell no one of the new knowledge he has acquired.

I greatly enjoyed this book. With many of the conversations between the various sinners and the residents of Heaven I could see myself and my vices within them. C.S. Lewis manages to expertly capture the sins and flaws of man within each interaction. So much so that it is all so plainly relevant to today despite being published in 1945. If you are looking for a book that forces you to look inward and challenge your own actions and thoughts I highly recommend spending an afternoon to read through it.
