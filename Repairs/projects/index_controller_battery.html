Project Index Controller Battery

One day I picked up my Index controller to play some Beat Saber (one of the greatest VR games ever made) only to find that my right hand controller had died. Strange but I plugged it in and returned an hour later only to find that it turned on for about a minute on low battery before shutting off. After testing the charging cable I realized that the most likely culprit was the battery.

I found this handy guide to follow on the website iFixit. To get another battery I decided to salvage one from an old left hand controller I kept sitting around for parts. The guide was simple enough to understand but more difficult to execute as Vale had stuffed the Index controller full of various sensors to track finger movement. The goal was to reach the battery in the center of the controller without damaging any of the delicate ribbon cables, force sensors, capacitive sensors, or power board.

I managed to remove the batteries of both the spare left hand and current right hand controllers. The next issue was that the batteries were unique to the left and right hand controllers. I ended up swapping out what I could from each controller to allow the working left hand battery to fit into the right hand controller. Before reassembling the controllers I charged the right hand controller with the new battery for ten minutes and turned it on watching as it lasted for almost half an hour.

Finally after successful reassembly my right hand Index controller was repaired and in working order.

This project is in stage 6
